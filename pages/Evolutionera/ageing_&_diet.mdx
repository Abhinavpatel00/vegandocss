### What is Ageing?

Ageing is a natural process that occurs in all living beings, including humans. It refers to the gradual decline in physical and cognitive abilities that accompanies the passage of time. Ageing is a complex phenomenon that involves several biological processes, including cellular damage, oxidative stress, and inflammation. These processes, over time, lead to the accumulation of damage in cells and tissues, ultimately resulting in age-related diseases and functional decline.



Now that we've discussed ageing let's move on to Anti-Ageing. Anti-ageing refers to the methods, products, and practices that aim to slow down or reverse the ageing process. Although the term 'anti-ageing' may seem like a modern-day concept, humans have been searching for the fountain of youth for centuries. From ancient herbal remedies to modern-day cosmetic procedures, we've tried everything to delay the inevitable effects of ageing.

### The After-Effects Of Ageing

The effects of ageing are far-reaching and affect nearly every aspect of our lives. As we age, our organs begin to function less efficiently, and our bodies become more prone to illness and disease. Common age-related conditions include arthritis, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and dementia. Ageing also affects our physical appearance, with wrinkles, age spots, and sagging skin becoming more prominent as we get older.

For example, a study published in the Journal of the American Medical Association found that individuals who consumed a diet high in animal products had a higher risk of developing heart disease, cancer, and overall mortality compared to those who followed a predominantly plant-based diet.

Another study published in the journal Aging found that the consumption of red meat and processed meats was associated with a higher risk of cognitive decline and dementia. These findings suggest that non-vegan food may contribute to ageing by promoting inflammation and oxidative stress, two processes that are closely linked to the ageing process.

Antioxidants, found in plant-based foods, neutralize free radicals and help to protect against their damaging effects.
