# Veganism MYTH Buster 

Welcome to our page all about the myths and misconceptions about veganism! Here, we'll be setting the record straight on some of the most common misconceptions and providing accurate and evidence-based information.



## Vegans don't get enough protein


Fact: Protein is present in almost all plants, including vegetables, fruits, grains, and legumes. As long as you are eating a variety of plant-based foods, you are likely getting enough protein. In fact, many plant-based sources of protein such as beans, lentils, and tofu, are excellent sources of protein and also contain important nutrients such as fiber and vitamins.

## Veganism is expensive

Fact: A vegan diet can be as expensive or as cheap as you want it to be. You can eat a variety of fresh fruits, vegetables, grains, and legumes at a relatively low cost. These foods are also nutrient-dense and healthy. Additionally, there are many affordable options for vegan meat and dairy alternatives.



## Veganism is a new trend

Fact: The practice of veganism has been around for thousands of years and is not a new trend. Veganism has its roots in ancient Eastern cultures and has been practiced by individuals and groups throughout history. The modern vegan movement, which advocates for the ethical treatment of animals, began in the 1940s.

## Veganism is restrictive and boring

Fact: A vegan diet can be as varied and flavorful as a non-vegan diet. There are countless vegan recipes, ingredients, and products available today. With the variety of plant-based options, you can enjoy many dishes and flavors from different cuisines around the world.

## Veganism is only for young, healthy people

Fact: Veganism can be beneficial for people of all ages, including children, seniors, and pregnant women. Studies have shown that a well-planned vegan diet can meet the nutritional needs of people at all stages of life. It's important to work with a healthcare provider or a registered dietitian to ensure that your vegan diet is meeting your specific nutritional needs.

We hope this page helped to clear up any misconceptions you might have had about veganism and provided you with accurate and evidence-based information. Veganism is a lifestyle choice that can bring about significant positive change for yourself, the environment, and animals. As always, it's important to do your own research and consult with a healthcare provider or registered dietitian if you have any questions or concerns.
